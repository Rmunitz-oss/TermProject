Introducing Volumetric Techniques by Analyzing Bleaching Solutions

Goal

Develop techniques for volume measurements using a buret, pipet, and volumetric flask. Perform a dilution and then a titration to determine the percent sodium hypochlorite in a commercial bleaching solution.

Theory

The reaction of diluted bleach with KI solution in an acidic solution of HCl will reduce the OCl- to the Cl- ion. The excess I- ions will form I2 (colored brown), and the solution will then be titrated with a Na2S2O3 solution of known concentration. A bit of starch solution will be added when the titration mixture turns from brown to pale yellow, which will turn the titration mixture deep blue. The end point of the titration will be determined as the moment when the titration mixture becomes colorless. The amount of Na2S2O3 used will allow us to calculate the amount of I2 present, from which we can then determine the amount of Cl- ions, and thereby OCl- ions present in the diluted bleaching sample. Based on the ratio of one Na ion for each OCl- ion, the mass of NaOCl in the diluted bleaching sample can be calculated. Based on the mass of NaOCl, the volume of the bleaching sample used, and the density of the bleaching sample, the percent sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) in the commercial bleach can be determined.

Procedure

  1. Rinse the buret and funnel with 5 mL of Na2S2O3 solution twice.
  2. Fill the buret with Na2S2O3 solution higher than the 0-mL line. Eliminate air bubbles and record initial volume
  3. Rinse the pipet with bleaching solution twice.
  4. Use a pipet to transfer 10.00 mL of bleaching solution to a 100 mL volumetric flask.
  5. Fill the volumetric flask with distilled water, until the meniscus reaches the 100 mL calibration line. Mix the solution by inverting the stoppered flask fifteen times.
  6. Use a 100 mL graduated cylinder to transfer 100 mL of water to a 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask.
  7. Use a 100 mL graduated cylinder to transfer 10 mL of 10% KI solution to the 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask.
  8. Use a transfer pipet to add 25 mL of diluted bleaching solution to the 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask.
  9. Use a graduated cylinder to add 4 mL of 6M HCl solution to the 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask.
  10. Immediately place the flask under the buret and begin adding drops of the Na2S2O3 solution, while gently swirling the flask.
  11. As soon as the titration mixture turns pale yellow, add 1 mL of starch solution.
  12. Add Na2S2O3 solution dropwise until the blue color disappears
  13. Record the final buret reading to calculate the amount of Na2S2O3 solution needed for titration

Analysis

There were many sources of possible error in this experiment.

  • Possible sources of systematic error include:
    • Equipment was not properly cleaned or dried between uses, allowing tiny drops to tamper the measurements.
    • Diluted samples were not mixed perfectly, affecting the results of the titration.
    • The time it took to place the Erlenmeyer flask under the buret and begin titration from when the HCl was added allowed for I2 to be released by a reaction with O2 and affect the volume needed or titration.
  • Possible sources of human error include:
    • Measurements were not calculated perfectly when using the buret, transfer pipet, volumetric flask, or graduated cylinder.
    • Solution was over-titrated, leading to incorrect calculations of volume of Na2S2O3 solution needed for titration.

The experiment was only executed once to completion, so we were unable to compare data or calculate a mean percent. Two titration solution were accidentally over-titrated and were not included in the results, and we were unable to find a properly working transfer pipet to complete an additional titration. The extremely low percent of NaOCl determined seems to indicate a serious error in this experiment, possibly an issue regarding the sample of bleaching solution used. It may have been contamination, improper dilution, or another possible error that led to our results.

Conclusion

The percent NaOCl in commercial bleaching solution was determined to be 0.22 %.
