Estimating the Calorie Content of Nuts

Goal

Estimate the calorie content of various nuts by constructing and using a simple calorimeter.

Theory

The heat of combustion of the nut will be measured by using a calorimeter. Based on the specific heat of water, the mass of the water, and the change in temperature of the water, determine the amount of energy was released due to combustion will be determined. Dividing that result by the mass of the nut burned will then allow the amount of Calories per gram to be determined.

Procedure

  1. Construct a nut support stand using a paper clip and a cork, creating a raised support to hold the nut.
  2. Suspend an empty soda can 1-2 cm above the nut support stand.
  3. Measure and add 200.0 mL of room temperature water to the can.
  4. Insert a thermometer through the opening, taking care to ensure the thermometer does not touch the sides or bottom of the can.
  5. Record the initial water temperature.
  6. Measure and record the mass of the nut.
  7. Transfer the nut to the support stand and ignite the nut.
  8. Immediately after the nut stops burning, stir the water in the can with the thermometer.
  9. Record the water temperature.
  10. Allow the nut residue to cool, then measure and record its mass.
  11. Repeat steps 1-10 with various nuts.

Analysis

  • Possible sources of systematic error include:
    • Some of the heat of combustion may have been lost to the surroundings, and did not directly heat the water, affecting the calculations slightly.
    • The scale used to measure the mass of the nut may not have been not perfectly accurate.
  • Possible sources of human error include:
    • A minute quantity of nut residue was left on or around the support stand and was not measured, slightly decreasing the accuracy of the determined mass of the nut burned.

Conclusion

The heat energy produced from the combustion of cashews and almonds was calculated, allowing the average amount of calories per gram of each type of nut to be determined. Cashews were found to be 3.5 Calories per gram, and almonds 3.3 Calories per gram
